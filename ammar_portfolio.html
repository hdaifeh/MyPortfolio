Featured Publication

How do human behaviour and infrastructure interact in achieving biowaste policy objectives?

Journal of Cleaner Production, Volume 510 (2025), Article 145571
Authors: Ammar Hdaifeh, Sylvie Huet, Jean-Denis Mathias

This groundbreaking study explores the crucial dynamic between household behaviors and infrastructure development in biowaste management. Using a novel system dynamics modeling approach applied to the Valtom territory in France, the research reveals key findings: (1) infrastructure development alone is never sufficient to meet policy objectives because behavioral shifts are essential, (2) gradual behavioral intentions speed of change is necessary to meet objectives without infrastructure capacity constraints, and (3) trade-offs exist between different sorting behaviors. The study provides policymakers with insights into conditions necessary for achieving their objectives, accounting for dynamic interactions between human behaviors, population evolution, and infrastructure development.
